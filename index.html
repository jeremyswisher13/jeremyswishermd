Background

About Dr. Swisher

Dr. Jeremy Swisher is a Health Sciences Assistant Clinical Professor in the UCLA Department of Orthopaedic Surgery, Division of Sports Medicine. He completed his sports medicine fellowship at UCLA and his family medicine residency at Wake Forest University, where he earned the School of Medicine Research Award.

A former 4-year varsity starter and team captain for Austin College basketball, Dr. Swisher brings a unique athlete's perspective to his clinical practice. He graduated magna cum laude from Austin College with a degree in Biochemical Neuroscience and earned his medical degree from Texas A&M University College of Medicine.

Dr. Swisher specializes in musculoskeletal ultrasound, non-operative sports medicine, and injection-based therapies including PRP, hyaluronic acid, and corticosteroid injections. He is passionate about injury prevention, return-to-play decisions, and advancing the field through clinical research.

Clinical Focus

Areas of Expertise

Musculoskeletal Ultrasound

Diagnostic and therapeutic MSK ultrasound for real-time imaging of tendons, ligaments, muscles, and joints. Lead instructor for UCLA's musculoskeletal ultrasound teaching series.

Injection Therapies

Ultrasound-guided injection procedures including platelet-rich plasma (PRP), hyaluronic acid (HA), and corticosteroid injections (CSI) for chronic tendon and joint conditions.

Non-Operative Sports Medicine

Evidence-based, non-surgical management of sports injuries with emphasis on rehabilitation, load management, and return-to-play protocols for athletes at all levels.

Concussion Management

Evidence-based concussion evaluation and management, including wearable technology applications and return-to-play decision making for athletes across all sports.

Pickleball + Basketball Medicine

Specialized expertise in pickleball and basketball injuries, prevention strategies, and return-to-play. Published researcher on injury epidemiology in both sports.

Orthobiologics

National course lecturer on orthobiologic treatments and regenerative medicine approaches for tendon, ligament, and joint pathology in athletes.
